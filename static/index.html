Compare multiple simulation scenarios side by side.

1. Monod Kinetics

μ = μmax · S / (Ks + S)

2. Haldane Inhibition

μ = μmax · S / (Ks + S + S²/Ki)

3. Temperature Effect

μ(T) = μ20 · θ(T-20),  θ ≈ 1.07

4. DO Limitation

μ = μmax · S/(Ks+S) · DO/(KDO+DO)

5. AOB/NOB Model

NH₄⁺ → NO₂⁻ (AOB): dNH₄/dt = -μ·XAOB/YAOB
NO₂⁻ → NO₃⁻ (NOB): dNO₂/dt = μ·XAOB/YAOB - μ·XNOB/YNOB

6. Kinetic Orders

Zero: C = C₀ − kt  |  First: C = C₀e−kt  |  Second: 1/C = 1/C₀ + kt

7. Free Ammonia / FNA

FA = NH₄·10(pH−pKa) / (1 + 10(pH−pKa))
FNA = NO₂ / (1 + 10(pH−pKa))

8. Oxygen Demand

O₂ = 4.57 · ΔNH₄-N  |  Air = O₂ / (0.23 · 0.20)

Model Assumptions

Completely Mixed Reactor

Uniform concentration throughout the reactor volume at all times.

Constant Temperature

Reactor operates at a fixed temperature throughout the simulation period.

Constant DO

Dissolved oxygen concentration remains constant (sufficient aeration).

Monod Kinetics

Microbial growth follows Monod saturation kinetics with respect to substrate.

No Mass Transfer Limitations

Substrate and oxygen transfer rates are not limiting.

Constant pH

pH is buffered and remains constant throughout the simulation.
